About Usability

Usability is elusive because actually, it does not exist.

What really exist are defects. An application is considered more usable that other when it contains less defects.

A defect is any annoying thing that happens or that the users have to do in order to reach their goals, that does not add value to said goals.

For example having to wait for a page to render is a defect: the users have no choice but to wait, the wait adds no value, and if it weren't there the applicacion would be more usable.

This is a known defect, and there are technical approaches to mitigate it. Reducing the wait makes the application better, more usable.

Other usability defects are of the kind that confuse the users, or enrage them.

A defect that might confuse the user is inapropriate labeling, a defect that might enrage users is to allow them to fill a complex form and to tell them that the session is void only after they submit it.

In this context, "confusing the users" does not mean driving them totally crazy up to the point of suicide. It is enough to make them reread a label just to check if the previous take was right or not, making them lose focus for a fraction of a second.

Usability defects do not mean that the application can't be used to fulfill its purpose, it might be functionally right.

An application is more usable when it has less of this kind of defects. It is not possible to enumerate all of them, but a usability experrt can identify them and suggest changes. Or better, design the interaction without such defects.

This report is about usability defects apparent in the Entropy 5 application, as of the moment when each report was written.

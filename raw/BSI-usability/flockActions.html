BSI Usability

BSI Actions instead of icons

In the screen part shown the last two columns display repeated icons that, due to their regular repetition and the visual preeminence of images over text are much more eye-catching than the actual content of the grid, despite being less significant, and despite the fact that they are seldom used.

"Seldom used" means that every single icon will be used but a few times along its lifespan, that could be many years.

Also, over time more actions might rear adding even more columns.

I suggest that the icons be replaced by an actions artifact capable of containing as many concealed actions as needed like the "Actions" dropdown found in some Entropy grids designed after GMail´s "More ▼" button.

We could label the column "Actions" or avoid labeling it, and use a single icon, like for example a wrench or a cog (a single one, of course) like in the example at the left.

When clicked, or better upon the hover event, the list of actions unfolds showing both icons and text for each of all the actions.

This would also allow for having different sets of actions for specific records, like not allowing to edit records related to core or otherwise freezed properties.

Instead of having two icons column (a number that might grow in the future) the UI might look thus, using a cog icon to hide all the appropriate tools and clearing the not-enabled indicators:

Clicking the cog (or whatever). or merely hovering it, shall display a menu with all the available actions for the corresponding record. If over time the number of operation frows, or the application incorporates the ability to choose additional actions, still the UI remains not-so-cluttered.

Also, the "Enable" label is wrong, it should be "Enabled" because it refers to a status (enabled or not), not an action (enable).

in the column labeled "Enable", the disabled records should display nothing. This way it is much easier to tell the disabled records out of the whole.

In the edition sub-form the situation is even worse.

The enable action is implemented by a dropdown, while it must be a checkbox. In addition to the advantage of checkboxes that are operated with a single mouse click, being a checkbox conveys a message of binary option while a dropdown suggests that there are more statuses in addition to enabled and disabled.

If it is a requirement that the "Status" and "EnableD" words both be present an the same time, then still use a checkbox and display the Enabled or Disabled words depending on its status.
